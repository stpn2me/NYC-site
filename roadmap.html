ROADMAP

Technical & Community Roadmap

Our development team is comprised of community members volunteering their time to execute this roadmap. Ideas are shared openly among the community and strategy is determined through consensus of the development team.

2018 Goals:

Q1:

  • On-board domestic and foreign merchants to accept NewYorkCoin for goods and services.
  • Implementing NewYorkCoin wallet updates:
    • Updating newyorkc-qt wallet code to remove unnecessary alert systems.
    • Removing errant code lines which inadvertently trigger anti-virus alarms.
  • Auditing current NewYorkCoin source code:
    • Progress can be tracked at https://github.com/NewYorkCoin-NYC.

Q2 / Q3:

  • Continue to on-board domestic and foreign merchants to accept NewYorkCoin for goods and services.
  • Building a lightweight wallet to co-exist with the full node, eg electrum.
    •
  • Constructing a NewYorkCoin inflation-reduction plan in order to balance the need to maintain zero or near-zero transaction fees while adequately incentivizing miners to solve blocks on the NewYorkCoin blockchain.
  • Performing NewYorkCoin blockchain threat analysis and developing patches to improve resistance to 51% attacks, such as:
    • Advanced Checkpointing
    • Digishield
    • Merged Mining
    These changes, which have been deemed necessary by the development team for the longevity and security of NYCoin, will require a hard fork in the near future.

Q4:

  • Development: NewYorkCoin hard fork took place on October 28th. With this hard fork, triggered on block 4,800,000, the following was implemented:
    • The old Kimito Gravity Well was replaced by Digishield as a Difficulty Algorythm: Digishield adjusts the difficulty on the chain for finding new blocks in a better way to maintain the 30sec bock time.
    • Merged Mining (AuxPoW): allows mining pools to mine NYCoin together with other coins on their mining pool infrastructure.
  • Development: Further optimisation of the NewYorkCoin core code to stabilize the network and security of the coin:
    • Advanced Checkpointing (not yet fully implemented in the previous codebase updates)
    • Updating QT Wallet dependencies like LevelDB and BerkeleyDB
    • Improve performance: Lower the memory utilisation, improve blockchain synchronisation speed.
  • Development: (Re)Building a lightweight wallet to co-exist with the full node, eg electrum, to work with Digishield/MergedMining.
  • Development: Building a newyorkcj or comparable bitcoinj library to achieve the following:
    • Multiplatform mobile and desktop wallets,
    • BitTeller® ATMs at various NewYorkCoin community merchants’ locations.
  • Marketing: Continue to on-board domestic and foreign merchants to accept NewYorkCoin for goods and services.
  • Marketing: Focus on listing on top exchanges on Coinmarketcap, including a DEX.

2019 Goals:

Q1:

  • Continue to on-board domestic and foreign merchants to accept NewYorkCoin for goods and services.
  • Partner with existing and future altcoin merchant payment solutions to ease NewYorkCoin adoption friction.
  • Updating NewYorkCoin source code to introduce: SegWit and future support for the Lightning Network.
  • Exploringatomic swap on-chain capabilities on the main network.

Q2:

  • Continue to on-board domestic and foreign merchants to accept NewYorkCoin for goods and services.
  • Implementing support for smart contracts through Merklized Abstract Syntax Tree (“MAST”) as proposed by the Bitcoin community.
  • Develop proprietary NewYorkCoin merchant integration solution.
